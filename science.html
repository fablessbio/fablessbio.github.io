Science

Reading, writing, and editing the code of life.

Biological sequence behaves like natural language: a finite alphabet, recurring words, deep grammar. fablessBio trains foundation models on that language to predict, design, and engineer biology — from single genes to whole organisms, seen in the light of evolution.

DNA / RNA

Genome foundation model

Deciphering the code of life begins with sequencing, gene prediction, and functional annotation. We treat genomes and transcriptomes as text and train large sequence models that learn this grammar directly from data — across organisms ranging from bacteria to fungi to insects.

The approach is grounded in genome-scale programs: members of our group have contributed to the 1000 Fungal Genomes Project with the Joint Genome Institute and continue to consolidate the fungal genome universe. That depth of annotated sequence is the training ground for models that find genes, regulatory elements, and novel short open reading frames others miss.

  • Gene prediction and functional annotation at genome scale
  • Novel coding and regulatory element discovery
  • Microbiome, metagenome, and pan-genome representation learning
Protein

Protein foundation model

All matter is built from a finite set of elements; likewise, the innumerable proteins of the protein universe belong to a finite set of folds, decomposable into recurring folding units. We learn these building blocks and how they recombine, then use them to design and engineer biomolecules toward a target function.

Structure determines function. By pairing learned sequence representations with structural priors, our models move from describing the protein space to proposing new points within it — novel enzymes and binders that nature has not yet sampled.

  • Structure-aware representation of sequence and fold
  • Enzyme and biomolecule design from evolutionary principles
  • Mapping where proteins are born, innovate, and evolve
Genomic LLM

Biosequence language model

Language models such as GPT and BERT transformed natural language processing. Because biostrings share the structure of natural language, the same architectures transfer to biological sequence. We build a unified genomic LLM that reasons over DNA, RNA, and protein within one representation.

A shared model lets signals cross modalities — a genomic context informing a protein prediction, and the reverse — which is where much of the useful biology lives.

MSIT · National GPU Program

Training at national scale

fablessBio is a participant in the National GPU Support Program operated by the Ministry of Science and ICT (MSIT) of the Republic of Korea. The program provides the large-scale GPU compute required to train and evaluate our genomic and protein foundation models, supporting research that would otherwise be out of reach for a team of our size.
